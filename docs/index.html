Your sims' phone, finally interesting.

Every call and text reads your save state before a word gets written. Traits, mood, career, family role, who lives with them. Live weather where each sim is standing. Recent milestones, parties you both attended, the in-person chat you had yesterday, group texts you were both in, freeform notes you've written about the sim. All of it lands in the prompt.

So your dad opens with "hey kiddo, how's the hospital?" because he knows you're on the doctor career. Your ex mentions the funeral you both went to last week. Your best friend brings up the storm you're stuck in. And when you tell someone you need space, the mod picks that up — the next reply from them is a boundary conversation, not another cheerful check-in.

A flirty teen texts in lowercase with dramatic slang. An elder writes long, warm paragraphs. A grandparent dotes; an enemy sneers. A parent never opens with "hey man"; a Goofball cracks jokes mid-call. The voice fits.

What makes it different

There are other AI mods for The Sims 4. Llamafone is mine — three opinions baked in:

📞

Phone-first, not AI-everywhere

AI everywhere in The Sims 4 gets overwhelming fast — every NPC turning into a chatbot, every interaction generated. Llamafone keeps the AI on the phone, where it actually makes sense. Your sims still act like sims. Their calls and texts just stop being canned.

⚡

Lightweight — zero extra installs

Drop three files into your Mods folder, paste your API key into the config, and you're done. Llamafone is one .ts4script (~130 KB), one .package, and a config file — same shape as any other Sims 4 script mod. No companion process, no extra installs, no second-party dependencies.

🌳

Built for (and by) legacy players

I'm a legacy player. This is the mod I wanted for my own save — and I'm building it to be the best version of that for people who play the way I do.

What it does

Llamafone is a phone mod first. Calls and texts are the headline; everything else is a bonus.

Phone Calls

Random sims call you in character — your kid's friend, your boss, your ex. Each voice shaped by traits, age, mood, and your shared history.

Text Messages

Quick check-ins, group drama, gossip. Hit Reply on the popup and they keep the conversation going — full back-and-forth, in character.

Auto-Events

Sits quietly in the background and triggers incoming calls and texts while you play. Set the interval, set the mix, forget about it.

…and more

Other things the mod can do when you want a break from the phone.

Story Updates

Random Events

Promotions, fights, weird neighbors, family secrets — always actionable, never generic.

Storylines

Multi-session 3-act arcs with concrete gameplay goals. Pick a theme or let the AI pick.

One mod, every voice.

Same code, totally different writing — the AI adapts to each sim's age, traits, and how they feel about you. Here are real texts from the same save:

Now in your sim's phone (v2.0)

No cheat console required for everyday use. Call, text, and tweak settings from the in-game phone the way your sim would.

Group texts (v3.4)

Send one message to 2–4 sims at once. Each recipient replies in their own voice, staggered like real texts arriving over a few minutes. Every reply sees what the others just said — so responses stay distinct instead of a chorus of "same." The dialog title and the reply prompt both list the full group roster, so you always know who you're talking to.

A briefing call at group creation (using the default model) synthesizes each participant's voice and their web of relationships, then gets cached for every subsequent reply — so per-turn cost stays sane even over long threads. Persisted per-save in GroupTexts.json so quitting mid-thread doesn't lose it.

Per-relationship contact preferences (v3.4)

Alice broke up with Chad. She wants him to stop calling her every hour. She doesn't want him gone from the game — just from her phone. Bob (her brother) is still friends with Chad and should keep getting his texts. This is what per-relationship preferences are for: mute, "asked for space," or favorite — scoped to a specific household member's view of a specific contact.

The paused ("asked for space") state is the interesting one. Instead of blocking the contact, it drops their auto-event rate to 20% and tells the AI you asked for space — so their response is driven by who they are. Also: the mod auto-detects distance signals in messages ("leave me alone," "we're done," "need space") and sets the state for you.

It knows what's actually going on.

Every call and text is filtered through what's true in your save right now — not generic AI fill.

Geography aware

If a sim lives in your world, they'll suggest grabbing coffee. If they live in San Myshuno and you're in Willow Creek, they keep it long-distance — no "come over" from across the map.

Weather aware

Live read of the weather where each sim actually is — light rain, blizzard, heatwave, thunderstorm. Dramatic weather comes up in conversation; routine weather stays background. Off-world callers know THEIR climate too, so Sulani friends won't pretend it's snowing during a globally-Winter season.

Life events show up

Sim quit a job, got promoted, had a baby, got married, aged up? The mod catches it automatically and your friends bring it up — "heard you quit, you good?" — within days of it happening.

Parties, weddings, holidays

Sims 4 drops calendar events from the UI the moment they end. Llamafone snapshots them. Text a guest the morning after a house party — "great party last night." Text your ex the day of a funeral you both attended. Love Day, Winterfest, and vanilla holidays land with their real names, not internal IDs.

Recent hangouts remembered

When your sim actually chats, flirts, argues, or kisses another sim in-game, the mod logs it. Next time you text or call, the AI knows you saw each other in person — so the opener is "great catching up earlier," not generic small talk. Uses the same Sims 4 relationship bits (Just Chatted, Just Kissed, Social Context) the game itself tracks.

Family roles override traits

Your Outgoing Adventurous dad doesn't text you like a frat bro — he texts like your dad. Parents sound parental, siblings tease, spouses get intimate, grandparents ramble warmly. Traits are interests, not voice.

Friendship tone is law

Friends are warm. Acquaintances are polite. Enemies are openly hostile — no "rooting for you 💕" from someone who hates your guts. The current relationship beats anything from old chat history.

Your notes, your context

Write a freeform note on any contact — "kid's teacher," "asked for space after breakup," "boss's boss," "childhood best friend." Surfaced in every prompt for that pair, weighted heavily, so the AI stays in character on nuance the game itself can't label. Per household member — Alice's notes stay Alice's.

Boundaries carry through

Tell a sim "leave me alone" or "we're done" and the mod auto-detects it — flips them to muted or paused for that household member. Or set it manually in the Contacts UI. Every subsequent AI prompt for that pair leads with the boundary, not with warmth. A love-heavy ex ignores it and keeps calling; a decent friend apologizes; a mean one guilt-trips. The drama plays out based on who they are.

Group thread memory

When Alice later texts Sarah 1:1, the AI knows they were both just in a group text with Bob and Kate. The last few turns from that group thread land in the prompt — so 1:1 conversations reference shared context like real friend groups do. Runs both directions, capped to recent threads only.

Plausible contacts only

Sims on the lot don't randomly call you from the next room. A teen acquaintance doesn't suddenly text an elder unless they're family or close friends — cross-generational pairings stay grounded.

Realistic reply delays

When you text a sim, they "think" for a few seconds before replying — close friends fast, lazy or hostile sims drag. No more instant-psychic responses.

Get started

  1. 1

    Download the mod

    Grab the latest release from the downloads page. You need three files: Llamafone.ts4script (the script), Llamafone.package (the phone UI tunings), and llamafone.cfg (the settings file).

  2. 2

    Pick an AI provider and get a key

    Llamafone works with four different AI providers — pick whichever fits your budget, preferences, or paranoia. You only need credentials for the one you pick. Costs run about 25 cents to a dollar per typical session on the cloud providers; Ollama is free because it runs entirely on your computer.

    🧡 Anthropic Claude recommended

    What the mod was built and tuned against. Best writing quality at the price.

    1. Go to console.anthropic.com and sign up
    2. Verify email/phone if asked
    3. Add a credit card under Settings → Billing and load $5 minimum (~1,000 in-game calls)
    4. Go to API Keys, click Create Key, name it "Sims 4 mod"
    5. Copy the sk-ant-api03-… key immediately — you only see it once

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = claude

    💚 OpenAI (GPT)

    Works with any GPT model — gpt-4o, gpt-4o-mini, gpt-4-turbo.

    1. Go to platform.openai.com/api-keys and sign in (or sign up)
    2. Add billing under Settings → Billing; load credit ($5 minimum)
    3. On the API Keys page, click Create new secret key
    4. Copy the sk-… key — only shown once

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = openai + set default_model = gpt-4o-mini

    🔷 Google Gemini

    Has a generous free tier — you can play for a while without a card on file.

    1. Go to aistudio.google.com/apikey and sign in with a Google account
    2. Click Create API key (free tier requires no billing setup)
    3. Copy the AIza… key

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = gemini + default_model = gemini-1.5-flash (free tier) or gemini-1.5-pro

    🦙 Ollama (local — no API key) techy

    Runs on your own computer. No API key, no internet, no cost. More setup than the cloud options — you install Ollama, keep it running in the background, and pull a model yourself.

    1. Install Ollama. Make sure the tray icon stays running.
    2. Pull a model — e.g. ollama pull llama3.2:3b (small, fast) or a bigger one if your hardware handles it.
    3. Leave api_key blank.

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = ollama + default_model = llama3.2:3b (must match ollama list). Run llama.testconnection in the cheat console to verify setup.

    Treat your API key like a password. Anyone who has it can spend money on your account.
    • Save it in a password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, your browser) — not in a plain text file on your desktop.
    • Never paste it in Discord, Reddit, GitHub, screenshots, or any chat — even private DMs aren't safe long-term.
    • If it ever leaks, revoke it on the provider's dashboard and make a new one.
  3. 3

    Drop the mod files in your Mods folder

    Move both downloaded files into:

    Documents/Electronic Arts/The Sims 4/Mods/

    If you have Sims 4 mods already, you've been here before — same folder.

  4. 4

    Set your provider + API key in the config

    Open llamafone.cfg with any plain text editor (TextEdit, Notepad, VS Code — anything that saves plain text). Two lines to update near the top:

    provider = claude          # or: openai, gemini, ollama
api_key  = YOUR_API_KEY_HERE

    Replace YOUR_API_KEY_HERE with the key you copied. Examples per provider:

    provider = claude
api_key  = sk-ant-api03-Xy7…

# or

provider = openai
api_key  = sk-proj-…

# or

provider = gemini
api_key  = AIza…

# or (no key needed)

provider      = ollama
api_key       =
default_model = llama3.2:3b     # must match the model you pulled -- see `ollama list`
fast_model    = llama3.2:3b

    Save the file. If you're on Ollama, also confirm ollama_endpoint = http://localhost:11434 matches wherever your server runs. Ollama users can run llama.testconnection in the cheat console after loading a save — it verifies Ollama is reachable, the model is downloaded, and end-to-end generation works.

  5. 5

    Enable script mods & launch the game

    In The Sims 4, open Game Options → Other and tick both:

    • Enable Custom Content and Mods
    • Script Mods Allowed

    Restart the game. When the main menu loads, you'll see a notification popup confirming the mod is active.

    Inside any household, open the cheat console with Ctrl+Shift+C and type llama.status to see all the commands and confirm everything's wired up.

Common questions

Why use AI in The Sims 4?

For storytelling. The base game's phone calls and texts are limited dropdown options — useful for gameplay, repetitive for narrative. This mod replaces them with text written specifically for your household by the AI provider you configure (Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, or local Ollama).

If you're a storytelling-focused player who's tired of the same scripted lines and wants your sims' relationships to feel alive, this is for you.

Does this affect my saved game?

No. The mod doesn't modify your saves, sims, or world. It only reads game data (who lives in the household, their traits, mood, and relationships) and shows generated text in notification popups and phone dialogs.

Generated content is saved separately in Llamafone_Journal.json inside your Mods folder, so the AI can reference past interactions across sessions. Uninstalling the mod removes everything cleanly — delete the .ts4script, config, and journal files.

What does the AI know about my sims?

Just what's needed for the current command — names, ages, traits, mood, careers, aspirations, household members, and relationship history. Nothing personal about you, no real names beyond what your sims are named, no save file contents.

Your prompts go from your computer directly to whichever AI provider you configured — there's no middleman server. Each provider has its own data-retention policy: Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all state that paid API traffic is not used to train models. If you really don't want any data to leave your machine, use Ollama — it runs entirely locally.

Is my API key safe?

Yes. The key lives only in llamafone.cfg on your computer. It's never uploaded anywhere except to make the API call to whichever provider you chose. The mod doesn't have a server, doesn't phone home, and isn't open to anyone else.

That said, treat the key like a password — don't paste it in screenshots, Discord, or screen-shares. If you ever suspect it leaked, revoke it on your provider's dashboard and make a new one. (Or just switch to Ollama — no key, no exposure.)

Can I disable specific features?

Yes. Everything is opt-in. Auto-events (random calls/texts/stories while you play) can be toggled with llama.auto_events off in the cheat console. You can also edit llamafone.cfg to disable specific auto-event types, change interval/chance, or restrict which sims can call you.

If you don't run a command, nothing happens. The mod is dormant until you trigger it.

Does it work on Mac?

Yes — drop the .ts4script into ~/Documents/Electronic Arts/The Sims 4/Mods/ and it works identically to Windows. Same commands, same behavior.

How much does it cost?

Does this slow down the game?

No. All API calls happen on background threads, so the game keeps running while text is generated. You'll see a notification popup when the response arrives, usually within a few seconds.

What if I'm offline?

The mod needs an internet connection to call the AI provider. If you're offline, commands silently fail without interrupting your game — no crashes, no popups. Your sims still play normally; you just won't get AI-generated content during offline sessions.

Is this against the Sims 4 Terms of Service?

No. The Sims 4 supports script mods officially through its Mods folder and Custom Content settings. This mod uses the documented script-mod system and doesn't modify any game files. EA's stance on script mods is that they're allowed; they just won't support issues that occur while mods are installed.

What's new

Recent changes from the dev branch — the mod's actively maintained.

  1. July 17, 2026

    Hotfix: cache seeding + dialog verbiage (v3.4.1)

    A hardening pass and some copy cleanup on top of v3.4.0. Drop-in over v3.4.0.

    • Time-delta calculations were wildly wrong (600×). The mod's ticks-per-minute constant was 100 in every module that reasoned about in-game time -- but Sims 4's date_and_time.py actually uses REAL_MILLISECONDS_PER_SIM_SECOND = 1000 (so 60,000 ticks per sim minute, not 100). Every "N in-game days/hours ago" phrase across contact prefs, journal, past events, and interactions was off by 600×. An in-person contact from 1 sim hour ago rendered as "25 in-game days ago". Fixed the constant across all 5 per-save modules; existing stored ticks values are unchanged (they were correct raw game time) — only the interpretation was wrong.
    • Household members were rendering as "we saw each other X hours ago". Siblings and other sims living in the same household see each other constantly regardless of when the last Sims 4 relationship bit fired. Rendering "you two saw each other 6 in-game hours ago" for cohabiting sims read as absurd. Fixed by short-circuiting the in-person tag when both sims share a household id -- new tag says "you two live in the same household. You see each other constantly at home."
    • "Your mom" phrasing when the mom is shared. The mutual-reference rule said to use family roles from the RECIPIENT's perspective ("your dad", "your mom"), which was wrong when the mutual is family of both the sender AND the recipient (Luca calling Francesca about their shared mom Vivian). Added a SHARED-FAMILY EXCEPTION to the rule: when the mutual's family role is the same from both sides (both call her Mother, both call him Brother), use "our mom / our dad / our sister" or just "mom / dad". Never "your mom" when the mom is your mom too.
    • Group text replies going to the wrong player-turn. When a group thread had multiple player-turns (e.g. an invite from morning + a thank-you from afternoon), the AI could respond to the older topic instead of the fresh one -- so Vivian/Luca/Martha replied about "tomorrow at 5" when the player had moved on to thanking them for last night's party. Fixed by finding the most-recent player-turn in the thread and pinning it explicitly in each participant's reply prompt with a hard rule: "respond to THIS specific message, not any earlier topic in the thread even if it looks unresolved."
    • AI breaking frame with meta-commentary. When a contact was in "asked for space" (paused) state but had recent affectionate journal history, the AI would notice the contradiction and reply with something like "I need to pause here because the context contains a critical contradiction I can't resolve in-character..." — asking the PLAYER clarifying questions instead of writing the sim's actual message. Fixed by: (a) reworded the boundary-state prompt block to remove menu-of-options language that invited deliberation, and (b) added an explicit hard rule to the call/text/reply system prompts: never break frame, never explain, never ask the player questions, pick one interpretation silently and write ONE in-character message.
    • Migration save-back could nuke a good ContactPreferences.json. The write-back condition in contact_prefs._load intended to persist on-first-migration, but its guard ("_legacy_sims" in data or "pairs" in data) was always true because "pairs" got injected right before the check. Combined with a transient parse failure (partial write, encoding hiccup, antivirus file-lock), the recovery path could write an empty {"pairs":{}} back to disk, wiping real data. Fixed: only save-back when an actual "sims" → "_legacy_sims" migration happens, and refuse to write on parse failure at all (cache empty for the session; disk file left alone for recovery).
    • Dialog subtext promised behavior the mod doesn't do. The recipient picker and message-input dialogs said things like "Llamafone will craft the message once you tell it what to say" — misleading, because outgoing texts and calls send the player's typed message as-is; only the sim's reply is AI-crafted. Stripped the subtext from the sim picker, multi-select group picker, message-input, group-message-input, and reply-input dialogs. Titles carry the context; no need for over-explaining copy.
    • Success logging added to contact_prefs._load. Future weirdness leaves a trail: after each successful load, the log records the pair/legacy counts and filename. Errors were already logged; this closes the "silent success" blind spot.
    • README refreshed to reflect v3.4.0 features (group texts, per-relationship contact preferences, sim-world time, expanded context awareness) and the new llama.contact / llama.testconnection commands.
  2. July 12, 2026

    Group texts, per-relationship contact preferences, and better error messages (v3.4.0)

    Two big features and a bunch of polish. Drop-in over v3.3.1. Existing saves and configs untouched; the mod auto-migrates any pre-refactor contact preferences on first load.

    • Group texts. Send Text now uses a multi-select picker — pick 1 sim for a normal 1:1 text, or 2–4 for a group thread. Each recipient replies in their own voice with staggered delays, and every reply sees what the others just said (so replies stay distinct, not a chorus of "same"). The thread persists to GroupTexts.json in your save folder. Group briefing (one AI call at group creation) is cached and reused for every subsequent reply, so per-turn cost stays sane. Reply button on any group message fans out to the whole roster automatically — dialog title and reply input both say Group text with Sarah, Bob, Alice, and Kate so you always see who's in the group. Configurable size (default 4, hard-cap 8) and optional gentle "someone got busy" drop-off in later rounds.
    • Per-relationship contact preferences. Mute, "asked for space" (paused), or favorite (priority), plus a freeform note field — all scoped to a specific (household sim, contact sim) pair. Alice muting her ex doesn't affect Bob's phone activity with the same sim. The "paused" state is the interesting one: instead of blocking the contact, it drops their auto-event rate to 20% and injects a boundary note into every AI prompt for that pair, so their response depends on who they are. A love-heavy sim ignores it and keeps calling; a decent one apologizes; a mean one guilt-trips. All accessible from Phone > Social > Llamafone Settings > Manage contacts (in-game UI — no cheat console needed) or via llama.contact First Last muted|paused|priority|clear|note <text>.
    • Auto-detected distance signals. The mod scans every text and call (both incoming and outgoing) for phrases like "leave me alone," "we're done," "need space," "back off," "lose my number." When detected, it auto-applies the matching state to that (household, contact) pair. Priority-tier contacts are exempt; the auto-note preserves context so you can see WHY the mod reacted. Conservative on purpose — only whole-phrase matches, only one direction of change (never downgrades muted to paused, never overrides priority).
    • In-game time, not real-world dates. Preference state timestamps use Sims 4's services.time_service().sim_now so shelving the game for six real-world months doesn't rot the state. The AI prompt says "you asked for space earlier today" or "3 in-game days ago," matching what the sim would actually experience in-world.
    • Better error messages for Ollama users. The most common non-technical user issue was a raw "Network error: curl exited with code 7" popup when Ollama wasn't running. That's replaced with a step-by-step message pointing at the system tray, Start menu, and the ollama list Command Prompt check. New llama.testconnection command walks through the whole setup — confirms Ollama is reachable, lists installed models, verifies your configured models are among them (with exact ollama pull commands if not), then runs a tiny end-to-end generation to prove the pipeline works.
    • Group-text picker fix. Initial group text release had a picker that wouldn't accept multi-select — the client-side renderer keys off min_selectable = 0, not max_selectable_num > 1 alone. Fixed by matching MCCC's pattern; the picker now renders checkboxes correctly.
    • Timer + runtime state cleanup on save switch. Group text mid-round runtime state now clears alongside the reply-delay Timer cancellation when you load a different save — nothing from the previous save can fire into the new one.
  3. July 1, 2026

    Hotfix: reply chain + phone anchor (v3.3.1)

    Two bugs reported by users. Drop-in over v3.3.0.

    • Reply chain no longer dies after one response. A one-character typo in v3.2.0's generate_reply (variable named recipient_sim_id instead of main_sim_id) was raising a NameError on every Reply-button reply. The exception was being swallowed silently by except Exception: pass in the text-input dialog callback, so the mod appeared frozen — first reply worked (different code path), every subsequent one just did nothing. Fixed the typo. Also replaced the silent except: pass with actual error logging so the next silent-failure bug can't hide for weeks.
    • Incoming calls/texts now ring the right sim's phone. Two of the mod's five phone-dialog call paths (send_text and send_call) weren't passing the recipient's sim info at all — so the dialog fell through defensive fallbacks and ultimately anchored to whoever the player had selected. If a toddler was selected when this fired, the toddler's non-clickable phone appeared to ring and the message became inaccessible. Fixed both paths to pass the recipient explicitly, and removed the "fall back to whoever's selected" behavior entirely — every call path now has a deterministic recipient (from the household, the stored conversation, or the currently-played sim), so no fallback is needed.
    • Timer callbacks now log their errors. The reply-delay Timer (the "sim is thinking" pause before their response appears) runs on a background thread. Python's threading.Timer silently swallows any exception on that thread — so if the reply generator raised, the response just never came. Wrapped the callback body in try/except with logging so future Timer-side bugs surface instead of vanishing.
  4. June 30, 2026

    Sims remember the parties (v3.3.0)

    Past-event memory is finally working end-to-end, and built-in Sims 4 holidays now surface with their real names. Same per-save data layout as v3.2.0, drop-in.

    • Sims remember the party you both attended. Throw a house party, dinner, wedding, funeral, whatever — end it, then text a guest the next sim day. The AI now sees a Recent events you both attended: block in the prompt with the event and how long ago, so they'll open with "great party last night" or "thanks for coming to grandpa's wake" instead of generic small talk. Data lands in PastEvents.json in your save folder as each event ends.
    • The mechanism (for the curious). Sims 4 aggressively clears attendee data on situation teardown — sim_ids, guest_ids, host_sim_info, and even sit.id all get zeroed before any teardown hook runs. Solved with a background thread that polls every 15 seconds while a party is live: iterates all sims via services.sim_info_manager(), asks SituationManager.get_situations_sim_is_in(sim) which situations each sim is currently in, and stashes both the sim id and the situation id onto the situation as private attributes. Those private attributes survive the teardown clear, so when the situation is destroyed we still have the full attendee list and event id.
    • Love Day and other built-in holidays now show in upcoming events. Vanilla Sims 4 holidays (Love Day, Winterfest, Harvestfest, New Year's, etc.) have holiday_ids in the low-integer range that Sims 4's holiday_service.get_holiday_display_name() returns empty for. They were silently dropped from the calendar block. Added class-level fallbacks: try display_name tunable first, then a cleaned __name__ of the holiday class. Also strips Premade_Holiday_ prefix so the name reads as "Love Day" not "Premade Holiday Love Day".
    • Prettier event names in prompts. Situation_HouseParty now reads as "House Party" in the recent-events block; Situation_DinnerParty_Formal as "Dinner Party Formal", etc. Strips class prefixes and splits CamelCase.
