Every call and text reads what's actually happening in your save — who lives in the household, their traits, mood, relationships, where they are — and sends it to the AI as context. Nobody calls about a job they don't have. Nobody texts about a sim who doesn't exist. The phone writes for the people in front of it.

A flirty teen texts in lowercase with dramatic slang. An elder writes long, warm paragraphs. A grandparent dotes; an enemy sneers. A Goofball cracks jokes mid-call. The voice fits.

What makes it different

There are other AI mods for The Sims 4. Llamafone is mine — three opinions baked in:

📞

Phone-first, not AI-everywhere

AI everywhere in The Sims 4 gets overwhelming fast — every NPC turning into a chatbot, every interaction generated. Llamafone keeps the AI on the phone, where it actually makes sense. Your sims still act like sims. Their calls and texts just stop being canned.

⚡

Lightweight — zero extra installs

Drop three files into your Mods folder, paste your API key into the config, and you're done. Llamafone is one .ts4script (~130 KB), one .package, and a config file — same shape as any other Sims 4 script mod. No companion process, no extra installs, no second-party dependencies.

🌳

Built for (and by) legacy players

I'm a legacy player. This is the mod I wanted for my own save — and I'm building it to be the best version of that for people who play the way I do.

What it does

Llamafone is a phone mod first. Calls and texts are the headline; everything else is a bonus.

Phone Calls

Random sims call you in character — your kid's friend, your boss, your ex. Each voice shaped by traits, age, mood, and your shared history.

Text Messages

Quick check-ins, group drama, gossip. Hit Reply on the popup and they keep the conversation going — full back-and-forth, in character.

Auto-Events

Sits quietly in the background and triggers incoming calls and texts while you play. Set the interval, set the mix, forget about it.

If a sim lives in your world, they'll suggest grabbing coffee. If they live in San Myshuno and you're in Willow Creek, they keep it long-distance — no "come over" from across the map.

Picks up real events

Sim quit a job, got promoted, had a baby, got married, aged up? The mod catches it automatically and your friends bring it up — "heard you quit, you good?" — within days of it happening.

Family roles override traits

Your Outgoing Adventurous dad doesn't text you like a frat bro — he texts like your dad. Parents sound parental, siblings tease, spouses get intimate, grandparents ramble warmly.

Plausible contacts only

Sims on the lot don't randomly call you from the next room. A teen acquaintance doesn't suddenly text an elder unless they're family or close friends — cross-generational pairings stay grounded.

Friendship tone is law

Realistic reply delays

When you text a sim, they "think" for a few seconds before replying — close friends fast, lazy or hostile sims drag. No more instant-psychic responses.

Get started

  1. 1

    Download the mod

    Grab the latest release from the downloads page. You need three files: Llamafone.ts4script (the script), Llamafone.package (the phone UI tunings), and llamafone.cfg (the settings file).

  2. 2

    Pick an AI provider and get a key

    Llamafone works with four different AI providers — pick whichever fits your budget, preferences, or paranoia. You only need credentials for the one you pick. Costs run about 25 cents to a dollar per typical session on the cloud providers; Ollama is free because it runs entirely on your computer.

    🧡 Anthropic Claude recommended

    What the mod was built and tuned against. Best writing quality at the price.

    1. Go to console.anthropic.com and sign up
    2. Verify email/phone if asked
    3. Add a credit card under Settings → Billing and load $5 minimum (~1,000 in-game calls)
    4. Go to API Keys, click Create Key, name it "Sims 4 mod"
    5. Copy the sk-ant-api03-… key immediately — you only see it once

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = claude

    💚 OpenAI (GPT)

    Works with any GPT model — gpt-4o, gpt-4o-mini, gpt-4-turbo.

    1. Go to platform.openai.com/api-keys and sign in (or sign up)
    2. Add billing under Settings → Billing; load credit ($5 minimum)
    3. On the API Keys page, click Create new secret key
    4. Copy the sk-… key — only shown once

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = openai + set default_model = gpt-4o-mini

    🔷 Google Gemini

    Has a generous free tier — you can play for a while without a card on file.

    1. Go to aistudio.google.com/apikey and sign in with a Google account
    2. Click Create API key (free tier requires no billing setup)
    3. Copy the AIza… key

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = gemini + default_model = gemini-1.5-flash (free tier) or gemini-1.5-pro

    🦙 Ollama (local — no API key)

    Runs the AI on your own computer. No API key. No internet. No cost. Slower than cloud providers and quality depends on what model your hardware can run.

    1. Install Ollama for your OS
    2. In a terminal: ollama pull llama3.1 (or mistral, qwen2.5, gemma2, etc.)
    3. Make sure the Ollama app is running (it serves on http://localhost:11434 by default)
    4. No key — leave api_key blank in the config

    In llamafone.cfg: provider = ollama + default_model = llama3.1 (whatever you pulled)

    Treat your API key like a password. Anyone who has it can spend money on your account.
    • Save it in a password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, your browser) — not in a plain text file on your desktop.
    • Never paste it in Discord, Reddit, GitHub, screenshots, or any chat — even private DMs aren't safe long-term.
    • If it ever leaks, revoke it on the provider's dashboard and make a new one.
  3. 3

    Drop the mod files in your Mods folder

    Move both downloaded files into:

    Documents/Electronic Arts/The Sims 4/Mods/

    If you have Sims 4 mods already, you've been here before — same folder.

  4. 4

    Set your provider + API key in the config

    Open llamafone.cfg with any plain text editor (TextEdit, Notepad, VS Code — anything that saves plain text). Two lines to update near the top:

    provider = claude          # or: openai, gemini, ollama
api_key  = YOUR_API_KEY_HERE

    Replace YOUR_API_KEY_HERE with the key you copied. Examples per provider:

    provider = claude
api_key  = sk-ant-api03-Xy7…

# or

provider = openai
api_key  = sk-proj-…

# or

provider = gemini
api_key  = AIza…

# or (no key needed)

provider = ollama
api_key  =

    Save the file. If you're on Ollama, also confirm ollama_endpoint = http://localhost:11434 matches wherever your server runs.

  5. 5

    Enable script mods & launch the game

    In The Sims 4, open Game Options → Other and tick both:

    • Enable Custom Content and Mods
    • Script Mods Allowed

    Restart the game. When the main menu loads, you'll see a notification popup confirming the mod is active.

    Inside any household, open the cheat console with Ctrl+Shift+C and type llama.status to see all the commands and confirm everything's wired up.

Common questions

Why use AI in The Sims 4?

For storytelling. The base game's phone calls and texts are limited dropdown options — useful for gameplay, repetitive for narrative. This mod replaces them with text written specifically for your household by the AI provider you configure (Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, or local Ollama).

If you're a storytelling-focused player who's tired of the same scripted lines and wants your sims' relationships to feel alive, this is for you.

Does this affect my saved game?

No. The mod doesn't modify your saves, sims, or world. It only reads game data (who lives in the household, their traits, mood, and relationships) and shows generated text in notification popups and phone dialogs.

Generated content is saved separately in Llamafone_Journal.json inside your Mods folder, so the AI can reference past interactions across sessions. Uninstalling the mod removes everything cleanly — delete the .ts4script, config, and journal files.

What does the AI know about my sims?

Just what's needed for the current command — names, ages, traits, mood, careers, aspirations, household members, and relationship history. Nothing personal about you, no real names beyond what your sims are named, no save file contents.

Your prompts go from your computer directly to whichever AI provider you configured — there's no middleman server. Each provider has its own data-retention policy: Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all state that paid API traffic is not used to train models. If you really don't want any data to leave your machine, use Ollama — it runs entirely locally.

Is my API key safe?

Yes. The key lives only in llamafone.cfg on your computer. It's never uploaded anywhere except to make the API call to whichever provider you chose. The mod doesn't have a server, doesn't phone home, and isn't open to anyone else.

That said, treat the key like a password — don't paste it in screenshots, Discord, or screen-shares. If you ever suspect it leaked, revoke it on your provider's dashboard and make a new one. (Or just switch to Ollama — no key, no exposure.)

Can I disable specific features?

Yes. Everything is opt-in. Auto-events (random calls/texts/stories while you play) can be toggled with llama.auto_events off in the cheat console. You can also edit llamafone.cfg to disable specific auto-event types, change interval/chance, or restrict which sims can call you.

If you don't run a command, nothing happens. The mod is dormant until you trigger it.

Does it work on Mac?

Yes — drop the .ts4script into ~/Documents/Electronic Arts/The Sims 4/Mods/ and it works identically to Windows. Same commands, same behavior.

How much does it cost?

Does this slow down the game?

No. All API calls happen on background threads, so the game keeps running while text is generated. You'll see a notification popup when the response arrives, usually within a few seconds.

What if I'm offline?

The mod needs an internet connection to call the AI provider. If you're offline, commands silently fail without interrupting your game — no crashes, no popups. Your sims still play normally; you just won't get AI-generated content during offline sessions.

Is this against the Sims 4 Terms of Service?

No. The Sims 4 supports script mods officially through its Mods folder and Custom Content settings. This mod uses the documented script-mod system and doesn't modify any game files. EA's stance on script mods is that they're allowed; they just won't support issues that occur while mods are installed.

What's new

Recent changes from the dev branch — the mod's actively maintained.

  1. July 1, 2026

    Hotfix: reply chain + phone anchor (v3.3.1)

    Two bugs reported by users. Drop-in over v3.3.0.

    • Reply chain no longer dies after one response. A one-character typo in v3.2.0's generate_reply (variable named recipient_sim_id instead of main_sim_id) was raising a NameError on every Reply-button reply. The exception was being swallowed silently by except Exception: pass in the text-input dialog callback, so the mod appeared frozen — first reply worked (different code path), every subsequent one just did nothing. Fixed the typo. Also replaced the silent except: pass with actual error logging so the next silent-failure bug can't hide for weeks.
    • Incoming calls/texts now ring the right sim's phone. Two of the mod's five phone-dialog call paths (send_text and send_call) weren't passing the recipient's sim info at all — so the dialog fell through defensive fallbacks and ultimately anchored to whoever the player had selected. If a toddler was selected when this fired, the toddler's non-clickable phone appeared to ring and the message became inaccessible. Fixed both paths to pass the recipient explicitly, and removed the "fall back to whoever's selected" behavior entirely — every call path now has a deterministic recipient (from the household, the stored conversation, or the currently-played sim), so no fallback is needed.
    • Timer callbacks now log their errors. The reply-delay Timer (the "sim is thinking" pause before their response appears) runs on a background thread. Python's threading.Timer silently swallows any exception on that thread — so if the reply generator raised, the response just never came. Wrapped the callback body in try/except with logging so future Timer-side bugs surface instead of vanishing.
  2. June 30, 2026

    Sims remember the parties (v3.3.0)

    Past-event memory is finally working end-to-end, and built-in Sims 4 holidays now surface with their real names. Same per-save data layout as v3.2.0, drop-in.

    • Sims remember the party you both attended. Throw a house party, dinner, wedding, funeral, whatever — end it, then text a guest the next sim day. The AI now sees a Recent events you both attended: block in the prompt with the event and how long ago, so they'll open with "great party last night" or "thanks for coming to grandpa's wake" instead of generic small talk. Data lands in PastEvents.json in your save folder as each event ends.
    • The mechanism (for the curious). Sims 4 aggressively clears attendee data on situation teardown — sim_ids, guest_ids, host_sim_info, and even sit.id all get zeroed before any teardown hook runs. Solved with a background thread that polls every 15 seconds while a party is live: iterates all sims via services.sim_info_manager(), asks SituationManager.get_situations_sim_is_in(sim) which situations each sim is currently in, and stashes both the sim id and the situation id onto the situation as private attributes. Those private attributes survive the teardown clear, so when the situation is destroyed we still have the full attendee list and event id.
    • Love Day and other built-in holidays now show in upcoming events. Vanilla Sims 4 holidays (Love Day, Winterfest, Harvestfest, New Year's, etc.) have holiday_ids in the low-integer range that Sims 4's holiday_service.get_holiday_display_name() returns empty for. They were silently dropped from the calendar block. Added class-level fallbacks: try display_name tunable first, then a cleaned __name__ of the holiday class. Also strips Premade_Holiday_ prefix so the name reads as "Love Day" not "Premade Holiday Love Day".
    • Prettier event names in prompts. Situation_HouseParty now reads as "House Party" in the recent-events block; Situation_DinnerParty_Formal as "Dinner Party Formal", etc. Strips class prefixes and splits CamelCase.
  3. June 30, 2026

    Sims notice when you actually hang out (v3.2.0)

    Two new context features. Same per-save data layout as v3.1.4, drop-in.

    • Sims remember when you were just together. When your active sim actually chats/flirts/argues/kisses another sim in-game, the mod logs the encounter. The next time you text or call that sim, the prompt tells the AI "you saw each other in person recently" — so instead of generic small talk, they open with "great catching up earlier" or "thanks for the chat". Detection uses Sims 4's own short-term relationship bits (Greeted, Social Context, Just Chatted/Kissed/Argued/etc). The tag appears exactly once per burst of in-person contact between phone conversations, so it doesn't get repetitive.
    • Past-event memory for calendar events. Sims 4 drops calendar events (parties, weddings, funerals, dinners, holidays) from the UI the moment they end. The mod now snapshots them as they complete and surfaces recent shared events in phone prompts for a few in-game days — so the morning after a wedding you attended together, the AI can text about it. Hooks the game's BaseDramaNode completion callback (the same lifecycle point every calendar event class inherits from), so it works for player-planned events, holidays, drama-scheduler events, all in one place.
    • Contact's immediate family always shown as mutuals. The mutual-contacts block was randomising 4 mutuals per call — meaning a contact's own children/spouse/parents/siblings sometimes weren't in the list, and the AI would say "Who's Luca?" when Luca was their son. Now the contact's family is force-included in the mutuals list before random-fill; total cap raised from 4 to 8 so both family and friends fit. Family relationships (via _get_family_relationship) are checked directly so grandchildren, in-laws, and step-relations all count.
  4. June 30, 2026

    Hotfix: per-save folder names now match the .save filename (v3.1.4)

    v3.1.2/3 named per-save folders with the slot id formatted as decimal (e.g. Slot_00004373) when Sims 4 actually names the .save files with the slot id in lowercase hex (Slot_00001115.save for the same id — 0x1115 == 4373). The folder name didn't match the filename, and on every play session the mod could land on a different decimal value as the player's save state changed. v3.1.4 formats slot ids as hex to match the .save filename exactly. On first load after upgrading, the mod auto-renames your old decimal-named folder to the correct hex name so your journal/milestones history carries over. No manual migration needed.

  5. June 30, 2026

    Stability pass (v3.1.3)

    No new features — just hardening based on a code audit after v3.1.2. Safe to drop in; same data layout as v3.1.2, no migration steps.

    • Journal + milestones writes are now thread-safe. The mod runs several things on background threads (auto-events, milestone scans, reply-delay timers) that all read and write the same files. Added locks so concurrent writes can't race and lose entries.
    • Milestones writes are now atomic. Snapshots, milestone log, and per-contact references now use the same write-to-.tmp-then-rename pattern the journal already had. A crash mid-write can't corrupt the file anymore.
    • Phantom divorces stopped for good. v3.1.2's fix for the lazy-load relationship bug only kicked in when BOTH snapshots had the new "spouse was readable" flag. Snapshots that survived from v3.1.1 didn't have the flag, so the safety check defaulted to "yes diff" and could still fire a phantom divorce on the first scan after upgrade. The default is now "no diff" until a clean snapshot exists — at the cost of missing real divorces only on the very first scan after upgrade.
    • Reply-delay timers cancelled when you switch saves. If a sim was about to text you back in ~30 seconds and you quit to the main menu, the reply used to fire in the new save's context (wrong contact, wrong conversation). Pending timers are now cancelled when a new save loads.
    • Backfill won't misattribute ambiguous names. The opportunistic journal backfill (which stamps a sim_id onto legacy name-only entries when it learns the mapping) now refuses to backfill when two sims share a name — the legacy entries stay name-only rather than getting bound to the wrong sim.

    Per-save history, smarter replies (v3.1.2)

    ⚠️ IMPORTANT — your journal/milestones moved.

    Per-save data (journal, milestones, sim snapshots, milestone references) now lives in:

    Documents/Electronic Arts/The Sims 4/saves/Llamafone/Slot_NNNNNNNN__<your save name>/

    …instead of one shared file in your Mods folder. The folder is created automatically when you load each save. If you want to keep your existing v3.1.1 journal/milestones data, do this once per save AFTER updating:

    1. Launch a save in Sims 4. Wait for the loading screen to finish (the per-save folder gets created at that moment).
    2. Quit. Open Documents/Electronic Arts/The Sims 4/saves/Llamafone/ — you'll see a Slot_NNNNNNNN__<save name>/ folder for the save you just loaded.
    3. From your Mods folder, copy these files in and drop the Llamafone_ prefix: Llamafone_Journal.json → Journal.json, Llamafone_Milestones.json → Milestones.json, Llamafone_SimSnapshots.json → SimSnapshots.json, Llamafone_MilestoneRefs.json → MilestoneRefs.json.
    4. Repeat for every save you want history preserved on.

    If you don't migrate, each save just starts fresh — nothing breaks. llamafone.cfg and Llamafone_Settings.json stay in the Mods folder unchanged (those are global, not per-save).

    • Per-save data folders. Multiple saves no longer share one history. Load Save A, talk to Bella → her journal entries land in A's folder; switch to Save B and a parallel "Bella" there has her own clean slate. The folder is created the instant Sims 4's loading screen finishes (verified hook against the game's Zone.on_loading_screen_animation_finished — no polling, no surprise CPU).
    • Journal lookups by sim_id, not name. Two sims with the same name (Maxis premades, your own duplicates) no longer cross-contaminate. Entries store both the name (for display) and the sim_id (for lookup). Legacy entries opportunistically get their sim_id filled in as you interact with each sim — no migration script, the journal self-heals.
    • Reply prompt brought up to parity with the call/text prompt. When an AI sim replies to your message, they now get the same rule set their incoming messages have — full friendship-tier table, geography rule, Sims 4 time block, banned-filler rules, calendar event handling, aspiration name-drop ban, mutual plausibility check. Previously the reply prompt was abbreviated and several rules silently didn't apply on the reply path. The user prompt also now carries [GEOGRAPHY], [SEASON], [WEATHER] tags on replies just like it does on incoming calls/texts.
    • Outgoing calls/texts know who's calling. When YOU call or text someone via the phone UI or llama.send_text/llama.send_call, the prompt now includes the player's full recipient block — mood, career, aspiration, top-3 skills. Previously only the contact was described, so the AI had no idea who they were talking TO. (Bonus fix: outgoing-call path was using the incoming-call system prompt by mistake, which told the AI to initiate the call instead of answer it. Fixed.)
    • Elder sims actually text like elders. The age rule was a soft "Elder: formal, warm" line that the AI would override when a sim's traits (Geek, Bookworm, Genius, Programming 10) read as a "tech nerd" archetype — pattern-matching them to lowercase millennial voice. Now hardened: elders ALWAYS use proper capitalization and complete sentences, regardless of traits. Traits are interests, not voice.
    • "Been ages since we caught up" frame killed. The banned-filler list previously matched literal phrases like "it's been ages" but slipped past reworded versions ("been ages since we [verb]"). Now bans the whole frame — any opening about how long it's been since contact, including with an empty journal (an empty journal just means recent contact wasn't logged, NOT that this is your first contact in months).
    • Clubs and interests stop transferring between mutuals. The AI was reading Daniel's "Board" club listing and writing "you and Bob doing that board-game stuff" to a sim who wasn't in the club. New rule explicitly says each sim's clubs/career/skills are listed individually — assume nothing transfers.
    • Phantom divorce/remarriage milestones fixed. For sims outside the active household, Sims 4 loads the relationship tracker lazily — a startup scan could see zero targets, conclude "not married", then a later scan would see the real spouse and conclude "got married", firing a phantom divorce + remarriage pair. The detector now tracks whether the relationship read was reliable and skips the comparison when it wasn't.

The AI can now reference real save state on both sides of the conversation: your dad asks how the doctor track is going, your mom asks how the painting's coming along, your sister teases you about the promotion she heard about.

The old detector compared sim household-id to whichever family the player was currently controlling — so swapping households via Manage Households fired phantom move events for every sim in your save. Now tracks the sim's absolute household identity and only fires on real moves. Old bogus entries are suppressed.
    • Journal: no more silent data loss. Removed the 150-entry hard cap (the journal now grows unbounded — no more silently dropping history on long-running saves). Atomic writes prevent partial-write corruption. If a journal file ever becomes unreadable, it's renamed to a timestamped .bak BEFORE the cache resets, so no data is ever blindly overwritten. Any failure is logged.

    • Renamed to Llamafone. The mod is now called Llamafone. Same mod, new branding — phone tiles, settings panel, cheat commands (llama.*), config file, and notifications all rebrand in step.
    • Bring your own AI. Pick provider = claude | openai | gemini | ollama in llamafone.cfg and Llamafone routes every call/text/story through whichever you choose. Cloud providers (Claude, OpenAI GPT, Google Gemini) need an API key; Ollama runs locally on your computer with no key, no internet, no cost — use whatever model your GPU can handle.
    • Gemini's free tier covers most casual play. If you're cost-conscious, set provider = gemini + default_model = gemini-1.5-flash and play for free. New llama.testprovider cheat command fires a tiny round-trip so you can verify your config in 5 seconds before playing.
    • No more emoji glitches. Local AI models sometimes mangle emoji into garbage rectangles in the Sims 4 UI. The mod now strips Unicode emoji and text emoticons from every response before they hit the dialog box, and the prompts forbid them. Plain readable text every time.

What's next

Where the mod is heading. Not a contract — priorities shift based on what you all report and ask for.

  • Planned

    In-game event triggers

    Calls and texts that fire when something happens in your save. Get dumped → your best friend texts to check in. Get a promotion → mom calls to congratulate. Thunderstorm rolls in → your dad texts to make sure you're okay. Reactive rather than random.

  • Planned

    Per-contact preferences

    Block a specific sim from ever calling. Mark someone as "texts only, no calls." Set frequency per contact. Give your protagonist control over who can reach them.

  • Planned

    Custom relationship overrides

    Tell the mod about dynamics the game doesn't natively label. Mark a sim as your mentor, your daughter's older boyfriend, your estranged half-sibling, your childhood best friend, your work nemesis — and the AI will write calls and texts that reflect that, even when the in-game relationship data is just "acquaintance."

  • Planned

    Group texts

    Multi-sim chats where everyone replies in their own voice. Pick two or three sims, type the message, and watch the group chat unfold — siblings ribbing each other, the friend group planning a night out, parents coordinating around the kids. Each reply lands in character based on their relationship to you AND to each other.

  • Planned

    Dynamic moodlet intensity

    Messages that land hard leave bigger marks. A sweet text from your crush moves the needle more than a polite check-in; an ugly fight hits harder than a passive-aggressive dig. The mod reads the content and scales the resulting buff strength to match — no more identical "Texted a Friend" moodlets regardless of what was actually said.

  • Planned

    Standalone Llamafone phone app

    A dedicated icon on your sim's phone home screen — tap it and you're in Llamafone, with quick access to calls, texts, conversation history, and settings. No more digging through the Social menu. The Phone > Social tiles will stay too, but the standalone app makes the mod feel native alongside the game's other phone apps.

  • Planned

    Service NPC roles (butler, maid, etc.)

    When a hired butler, maid, babysitter, gardener, or repair tech calls or texts, the AI knows it. Butlers use formal, deferential language and refer to the household as the estate; maids talk about supplies and schedules; babysitters check in about the kids; gardeners talk plants, season, weather. The mod reads the household's hired-service records from the game's ServiceNpcService — so a butler ringing the doorbell, calling about laundry, or texting to confirm tomorrow's schedule lands in the right register.

  • Open
